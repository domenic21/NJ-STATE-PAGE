History

Atlantic City was incorporated on May 1, 1854, from portions of Egg Harbor Township and Galloway Township.[22] The city boundaries were extended to the mainland in 1870, taking in all of Absecon Island, which were further extended in 1896 and 1900, reaching the now-existing Atlantic City Expressway and Garden State Parkway. The city's acceptance of gambling as a means of providing revenue was the result of a 1970 statewide referendum on legalized gambling. The first casino opened two years later. Atlantic City lies within the Southern Shore region of New Jersey, with a population of 38,446,nestled along the Atlantic Ocean's coastline. Classified as an urban city, it embodies the energy and vitality characteristic of metropolitan areas. Beyond the casinos and entertainment, the city offers a mix of cultural attractions, shopping districts, and historical landmarks, contributing to its multifaceted identity as a destination that appeals to a diverse range of visitors. average income in Atlantic City reflects its status as a renowned tourist destination. In 2023, the median household income in Atlantic City was $47,643. While the city's income level is influenced by its vibrant tourism industry, it also faces economic challenges related to seasonal fluctuations. The presence of world-famous casinos and entertainment venues contributes significantly to the local economy and employment opportunities, making Atlantic City a hub of excitement and fun.
The median household income in Atlantic City was notably lower than the statewide median household income in New Jersey. In New Jersey, the median household income was around $85,000 to $90,000 per year, placing it among the highest in the United States. Meanwhile, in Atlantic City, the median household income was around $40,000 to $45,000 per year, significantly lower than the state average. (United States Census Bureau QuickFacts, n.d.) >
